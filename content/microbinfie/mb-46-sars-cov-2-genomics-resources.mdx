# SARS-CoV-2 Resources Update

Stay informed on the recent updates to SARS-CoV-2 resources with the latest bioinformatics and genomics tools. Recorded on 5 February 2021.

- **Microreact Project (COG Consortium):** [microreact.org/project/cogconsortium/](https://microreact.org/project/cogconsortium/)

These resources are essential for staying updated on the latest developments and methodologies in SARS-CoV-2 genomics and bioinformatics.

### Extra notes

- **Shift to SARS-CoV-2 Analysis**: The podcast highlights a significant increase in focus on the genomic analysis of SARS-CoV-2 due to the ongoing pandemic. It emphasizes tracking viral variants and analyzing genomic data to understand and control viral spread.

- **Resources and Tools for SARS-CoV-2**:
  - **Covariance.org**: A website cataloging SARS-CoV-2 variants and mutations, providing insights into the biological impacts of specific mutations.
  - **MicroReact**: A visualization tool that displays phylogenetic trees, timelines, and spatial distribution of variants. Recent updates include better visualization of variant distribution as a proportion of total samples.
  - **GISAID**: Launch of a CLI API submission tool to streamline and speed up the genomic data submission process.

- **Challenges in Data Sharing**: The podcast notes issues with data sharing, including research parasites and acknowledgment concerns, but stresses the importance of open sharing for public health.

- **Integration of Epidemiological Data**: Combining genomic data with epidemiological data to understand and predict the spread of variants, as demonstrated by initiatives involving global collaboration.

- **Training and Education Resources**:
  - **Clam Arctic Workshop**: An online course with resources like YouTube videos and assignments to train researchers in genomic analysis of SARS-CoV-2.
  - **CDC's COVID-19 Genomic Epidemiology Toolkit**: A resource to help epidemiologists and researchers understand phylogenetic trees and genomic data applications.

- **Shift in Methodologies**: Transition from using RT-qPCR as a primary method to more comprehensive genome sequencing, with Denmark highlighted as a leader in establishing rapid genomic analysis capabilities.

- **Community Efforts and Collaboration**: Emphasis on community collaboration with open sharing of protocols on platforms like Protocols.io and task forces to develop flexible methodologies.

- **Technical Innovations**: The emergence of new tools and workflows aimed at improving genomic data processing and visualization, ultimately aiding in the rapid detection and analysis of SARS-CoV-2 variants.
