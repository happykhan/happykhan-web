# Panel Discussion on Mobile Genetic Elements

## Overview

**Guest Chair:** Dr. Muhammad Yasir  
**Panelists:** Dr. Emma Waters, Dr. Heather Felgate, Dr. Andrew Page  

This panel discussion covers topics related to mobile genetic elements (MGEs), focusing on:

- Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)
- **Salmonella Typhi**
- **Staphylococci**
- Outbreaks and the role of MGEs

The discussion was recorded in front of a live audience of PhD students participating in the Microbes, Microbiomes and Bioinformatics doctoral training program at the Quadram Institute, Norwich, UK.

---

## Key Topics

### Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)
- Discussion on how MGEs contribute to the spread of AMR genes among different bacteria.
  
### Salmonella Typhi
- Examination of MGEs in the virulence and transmission of Salmonella Typhi.

### Staphylococci
- Analysis of the impact of MGEs on the genomic plasticity and pathogenicity of Staphylococci.

### Outbreaks and MGEs
- Exploration of the role MGEs play in microbial outbreaks, potentially affecting public health strategies.

---

The panel provided insights into current research and challenges in the field, underlining the importance of understanding MGEs in combating bacterial diseases.

The guest speakers on the MicroBinFeed podcast episode are Dr. Andrew Page, Dr. Emma Waters, and Dr. Heather Felgate.
