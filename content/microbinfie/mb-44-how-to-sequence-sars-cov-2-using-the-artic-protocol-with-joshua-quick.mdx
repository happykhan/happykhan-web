## How to Sequence SARS-CoV-2 Using the ARTIC Protocol

### Presented by Joshua Quick, University of Birmingham

This presentation was part of a joint ARTICnetwork & CLIMB-BIG-DATA workshop focusing on COVID-19 data analysis. The session was chaired by Nick Loman. Below are some useful links and resources related to the workshop and protocols discussed:

- [Workshop Announcement on Twitter](https://twitter.com/Scalene/status/1349402397249056779)
- [Primer Design Tool - Primal Scheme](https://primalscheme.com/)
- [nCoV-2019 Sequencing Protocol v3 (LoCost) - protocols.io](https://www.protocols.io/view/ncov-2019-sequencing-protocol-v3-locost-bh42j8ye)

These resources provide insights into the methods and tools used for sequencing SARS-CoV-2 with the ARTIC protocol, offering a deeper understanding of the data analysis processes during the COVID-19 pandemic.

### Extra notes

- The podcast discusses the success of a decentralized genomic surveillance network in the UK, which utilizes diverse methodologies to enhance microbial bioinformatics.

- **Amplicon Sequencing:** The ARTIC method, a form of amplicon sequencing, is highlighted for its cost-effectiveness and scalability, suitable for retrieving genomes in challenging conditions like high host-level contamination.

- **Technical Methodology:**
  - Utilizes multiplex PCR to generate amplicon pools, followed by native barcoding, and nanopore sequencing.
  - Challenges with high CT values in viral isolates led to a focus on reducing RNA usage and increasing throughput with multiplex PCR methods.
  - Primal scheme is a web-based tool aiding in primer design for multiplex PCR, accommodating diverse viral genome inputs.

- **Tools and Innovations:**
  - Primal Scheme: A web tool for designing primers, allowing users to upload reference genomes, and the tool designs primers covering genome diversity.
  - The tool can handle multiple reference inputs and optimize primer design, accommodating segmented viruses and bacterial panels in future updates.
  
- **Challenges and Solutions:**
  - Designing primers for highly diverse genomes remains challenging; the focus is on single viral strains due to the difficulty in finding conserved primers.
  - Issues like primer efficiency and amplification failure lead to "dropouts" in genome recovery, emphasizing the importance of primer design and optimization.
  - Stringent demultiplexing is important due to possible coverage differences in multiplexed samples.

- **Direct RNA Sequencing Limitations:**
  - The direct RNA kit requires high input RNA amounts, unsuitable for clinical settings unless additional steps are taken, impacting efficiency.

- **Community Involvement and Support:**
  - The Arctic Network offers protocols online and collaborates with companies like ONT, QIAGEN, and Illumina for protocol support and commercialization of related products.

- **Future Directions:**
  - Enhancements planned for the Primal Scheme to support segmented viral genomes and address bacterial genome challenges, particularly those with high GC content.

- **Discussion on Native vs. Rapid Barcoding:**
  - Native barcoding is preferred due to the high specificity of demultiplexing required in the presence of amplicon dropouts.
  
These insights highlight ongoing developments, technical strategies, and challenges within microbial bioinformatics, particularly focusing on virus genome sequencing methodologies and tool development to enhance genomic data analysis.
