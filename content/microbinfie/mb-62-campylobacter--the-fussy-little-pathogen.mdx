# Podcast Guest: Dr. Ozan Gundogdu

Welcome to today's episode where we have the pleasure of hosting Dr. Ozan Gundogdu. He is here to provide us with a crash course on the foodborne pathogen **Campylobacter**. If you've ever experienced an upset stomach after consuming undercooked chicken, Campylobacter, often referred to as "Campy," might be the culprit.

## About Dr. Ozan Gundogdu

Dr. Gundogdu leads the Foodborne Enteric Pathogen group at the **London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine (LSHTM)**. His team focuses on the study of physiology and pathogenesis of Campylobacter and other related enteric microorganisms such as **Listeria** and **Vibrio**.

### Extra notes

- **Microbial Bioinformatics Focus**: Dr. Ozan Gongadou's work emphasizes the use of next-generation sequencing tools in studying pathogens like Campylobacter. This includes whole genome sequencing, transcriptomics, and metagenomics to explore microbial communities and interactions.

- **Challenges in Studying Campylobacter**: The pathogen is described as being difficult to study due to its specific environmental needs and non-model organism status. It poses challenges in laboratory settings as it is very particular and hard to grow.

- **Genomics and Genetic Regulation**: The compact genome of Campylobacter (approximately 1.64 megabases) contains open reading frames with minimal non-coding sequences, which suggests efficient genetic regulatory mechanisms that allow survival in less-than-ideal conditions.

- **Emergence of Genome Sequencing**: The first whole genome sequence of Campylobacter revealed unexpected characteristics such as lacking classical secretion systems (like the type three secretion system) but potentially having a type six secretion system in certain strains.

- **Microbial Community and Environmental Influence**: Research indicates that environmental parameters, such as stocking density, significantly impact the chicken gut microbiome and the prevalence of Campylobacter, suggesting the complex interplay between microbial communities and their environment.

- **Bioinformatics and Data Analysis**: The field of microbial bioinformatics is instrumental for understanding complex genetic structures and regulatory mechanisms in pathogens like Campylobacter, providing insights into disease transmission and control strategies.

- **Vaccine and Treatment Development**: Despite technological advances, the conversation around developing vaccines for Campylobacter remains complicated due to the cost-benefit analysis and challenges in significantly reducing bacterial populations in poultry.
