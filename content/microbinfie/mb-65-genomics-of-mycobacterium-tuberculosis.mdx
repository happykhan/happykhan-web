# Exploring Mycobacterium Tuberculosis: Bioinformatics, Genomics, and Typing

We are continuing our series that delves deeply into specific microbial species. In this installment, we further explore Mycobacterium tuberculosis, with a focus on bioinformatics, genomics, and typing.

## Focus Areas

1. **Bioinformatics**:  
   Examining the applications of bioinformatics in understanding M. tuberculosis.
  
2. **Genomics**:  
   Analyzing the genomic sequencing and data that reveal the evolutionary and pathogenic profile of M. tuberculosis.
  
3. **Typing**:  
   Understanding the methods used to type and classify M. tuberculosis strains, aiding in tracking and controlling outbreaks.

Join us as we dive deeper into these topics with insights from our esteemed guests.

The speakers on the MicroBinFeed podcast episode on microbial bioinformatics include Dr. Susie Hingley-Wilson, who is a lecturer in bacteriology at the University of Surrey; Dr. Jani Best, a senior lecturer in microbial metabolism at the University of Surrey; and Dr. Connor Meehan, an assistant professor in molecular microbiology at the University of Bradford.
